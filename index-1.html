     The demographics of China demonstrate a large population with a relatively small youth component, partially a result of China's two-child policy. China's population reached the billion mark in 1982.
     In 2019, China's population stands at 1.418 billion,[1] the largest of any country in the world. According to the 2010 census, 91.51% of the population was Han Chinese, and 8.49% were minorities. China's population growth rate is only 0.59%, ranking 159th in the world.[2] China conducted its sixth national population census on 1 November 2010.[3] Unless otherwise indicated, the statistics on this page pertain to mainland China only; see also Demographics of Hong Kong and Demographics of Macau.- All content from site are from Wikipidia and pictures are from google.
